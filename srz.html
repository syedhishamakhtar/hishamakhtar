CARDIOLOGY

What cardiology is

A branch of medicine focusing on the heart and blood vessels.
It involves the medical diagnosis and treatment of cardiovascular diseases.
Cardiology is a sub-specialty of internal medicine.

Diagnostic tools and procedures

Electrocardiogram (EKG): Measures the heart's electrical activity.
Echocardiogram (Echo): Uses ultrasound to create images of the heart.
Cardiac catheterization: A thin tube is inserted into a blood vessel to access the heart.
Coronary angiogram: A type of cardiac catheterization to visualize arteries.
